The Honors Program

While Honors shows up on a transcript just like any other course, it is more than that. In the Honors program, students are split into groups of roughly twenty students. Each group of students because a section (A, B or C). Throughout one semester, students will read upwards of 25 books, with readings of up to 200 pages. Every Tuesday and Thursday, each section meets to engage in a three hour Socratic seminar on the required reading. Students are encouraged both to take notes during reading and during seminar.

The Honors program was a big part of my college experience. It was more than just a course and seminar was more than just a simple review of the book. In Honors I found myself grappling with questions that would impact my entire life such as "What is the good life?" or "What is happiness?". During almost every semianr I discussed these topics with my peers, and the program has shaped my life in many ways. Even though I came to study computer science at college, I found myself enthralled with the Honors program and gained so much from being a part of it. The goal of Honors is not only to challenge your own thinking but to hear a diverse array of opinions on and reactions to a text in order to come to a greater understanding of a topic as a group. So, it is important that I include the Honors program when it comes to my college experience because it was such a big piece of it.
